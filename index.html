Product Walkthrough

Words Please is a one-page iOS application. For ease of use, the screen is split into 3 main intuitive parts:

  1. The “setting” picture
  2. The “sentence constructor” in the middle
  3. The “sentence fragment” tiles in the bottom
The setting picture in the background allows the child to get a context of where they are and what they can do by restricting the tiles that may show up at the bottom. Whenever a child selects a tile, the tile chosen will fit into the middle constructor and become part of the sentence. Finally, when the sentence is complete, the child may press it to hear how it is said.

Design Principles

Accessibility: Different children have different needs, and the tools they use should reflect that. Words Please is designed for that purpose: tiles come with picture definitions, careful planning has been made to reduce all unncessary features, and the page is kept as minimal yet as functional as possible.

Simplicity: Even the youngest children need to feel independence. We need to have things we have control over. Things we know we understand. Words Please has no mistaken click that forces an adult's intervention. Actions have visual effects to make cause-and-effect intuitive. There are no settings to adjust for.

Delightfulness: Learning is easier when it's fun. Visual cues and a simple color scheme drive the application's design-- whilst tiles and sentences fit together like pieces to a puzzle. Not just to aid in communication, we want children to understand how pieces fit together and eventually internalize it on their own.

Product Philosophy

At the core of Words Please is the desire to help children not just communicate during times of need, but to encourage a growth towards independence. Our team's greatest goal for the product is that the child will eventually stop using it, because they can do it on their own.
